MUSEUM OF CANDY

Lollipops are a popular type of confectionery consisting of hard candy mounted on a stick. They come in various shapes, sizes, flavors, and colors, and some are filled with chewing gum or soft candy. The invention of lollipops is not attributed to a single person, as candies on sticks have been enjoyed for centuries in different cultures.

MUSEUM OF CANDY

The Museum of Candy's gumball exhibit offers a colorful journey through the history and fun of these iconic treats. Visitors can explore the origins of gumballs, see how they're made, and discover different flavors and varieties. With interactive displays and classic gumball machines, it's a playful and nostalgic experience for candy lovers of all ages.

Sprinkles are a colorful and fun addition to any sweet treat, adding a delightful crunch and a burst of color. Often used to decorate cakes, cookies, ice cream, and donuts, these tiny candies come in a variety of shapes, sizes, and colors. At the Museum of Candy, sprinkles are celebrated for their ability to transform ordinary desserts into festive, eye-catching creations, making every bite a little more magical.
